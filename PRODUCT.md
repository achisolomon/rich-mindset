# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hebrew-speaking adults participating in a financial mindset coaching program. They arrive with a coach's guidance and complete the questionnaire as a structured self-reflection exercise. They are not technically sophisticated — they expect a smooth, focused experience with no friction. When they finish, they want to feel seen and understood, with results clear enough to bring into a coaching conversation.

## Product Purpose

A 40-question Likert-scale self-assessment that maps a person's financial mindset across 12 categories (self-perception, time/money, growth, abundance vs. scarcity, financial strategy, etc.). It exists to surface blind spots and spark productive coaching conversations — not to judge or prescribe. Success means the person completes all questions honestly, understands their results at a glance, and arrives at their next coaching session with specific categories to discuss.

## Brand Personality

Warm, clear, empowering. Like a trusted coach who's direct but never harsh. Confident in its structure; gentle in its framing.

## Anti-references

- Corporate / bank-like: cold, formal, navy-heavy forms that feel like compliance paperwork
- Academic / clinical: dense, survey-instrument styling with no visual warmth or hierarchy
- Startup / tech dashboard: dark mode, chart overload, data-dense — wrong register for personal reflection

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — every question, label, and result must be immediately understood without re-reading
2. **One thing at a time** — the quiz flow should feel focused; no visual noise competing with the current question
3. **Results earn their complexity** — the breakdown page can be rich, but each element must earn its place by serving the coaching conversation
4. **Warm authority** — the tone should feel like a knowledgeable coach, not a bureaucratic form or a personality quiz
5. **RTL as a first-class citizen** — all layout, alignment, and flow must be designed for Hebrew, not retrofitted from LTR

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Users may be middle-aged adults unfamiliar with dense UI patterns. All interactive elements must be keyboard-accessible and have clear focus states. Minimum tap targets 44×44px on mobile. No reliance on color alone to convey meaning (especially in results scoring).
